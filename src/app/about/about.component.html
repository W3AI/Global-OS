The Math of Global Operating Systems

Smart Markets & Universal Church-Turing Machines

  • An Universal Church-Turing Machine selects instructions from a market of operators (service providers).
  • Each operation is wrapped by I/O lambda functions.
  • The Input lambda function selects from the project context the input arguments for the operation.
  • The Output lambda function updates the project context with the results of the operation.
  • The service providers are selected automatically based on availability, price and terms.
  • If several solutions are found they can be ranked on price, duration, etc before execution.
  • The combinatorics of services are thr proof that no better solution is possible at the time.
  • A combinatorial market of services is called a smart market.
  • Market players can chose to invest in the search of new cheaper and faster sequences of services.
  • A new, useful and in demand sequence is an innovation that can be exploited for profit in the market.
  • All services in the market can be ranked in a PTO directory to provide automatic patenting services.
  • The maturity of the market can be a function of the number of types of services available.
  • The speed of progress for a smart market is the speed by which new services (innovations) are added.
